Kiril Bangachev

I am a third-year PhD student at MIT, fortunate to be advised by Guy Bresler. My current research focuses on high-dimensional statistics. I am specifically interested in the interplay between the dimensionality of data, information, and computational efficiency. I have worked on problems related to high-dimensional random geometric graphs and noisy linear equations over finite fields.

Before coming to MIT, I was an undergraduate at Princeton where I was lucky to work with Matt Weinberg on problems in algorithmic game theory. I have also worked in combinatorics.
